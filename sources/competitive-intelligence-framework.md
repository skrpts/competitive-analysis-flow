## Competitive Intelligence Framework

This source document defines the methodology and standards used throughout the competitive analysis flow. All analysis produced by this pipeline conforms to these frameworks.

### Competitor Classification

**Tier 1 — Direct Competitors**
Products that compete for the same budget, solve the same core problem, and target the same buyer. A customer shortlisting your product would also shortlist these. Identifying direct competitors requires understanding the "job to be done" rather than just feature overlap.

**Tier 2 — Indirect Competitors**
Products that solve a related problem, serve an adjacent audience, or could be used as a substitute. These may not appear in the same buying process today, but market shifts could bring them into direct competition. Indirect competitors often represent future threats.

**Tier 3 — Emerging Threats**
New entrants, open-source alternatives, platform-native features, or adjacent products expanding into your space. These may not be viable alternatives today, but their trajectory matters. Monitor through funding announcements, product launches, and hiring patterns.

### Data Collection Standards

| Source Type | Reliability | Use For |
|-------------|-------------|---------|
| Company website and docs | High for features and pricing | Product capabilities, pricing, integrations |
| Press releases and blogs | High for announcements | Strategic direction, funding, partnerships |
| G2/Capterra reviews | Medium (selection bias) | User sentiment, common complaints, praised features |
| Job postings | Medium (inferential) | Strategic priorities, technology stack, growth areas |
| Social media and forums | Low-Medium (anecdotal) | Emerging issues, community sentiment, real-time signals |
| Financial filings | High (if public) | Revenue, growth rate, market share |
| Third-party market reports | Medium-High | Market sizing, trend analysis, industry benchmarks |

### Feature Comparison Methodology

**Scoring Scale:**

| Score | Definition | Evidence Required |
|-------|-----------|-------------------|
| Strong | Best-in-class capability | Specific feature documentation, positive reviews, recognised awards |
| Adequate | Meets market expectations | Feature exists and functions without major complaints |
| Weak | Below market expectations | Documented limitations, negative reviews, missing sub-features |
| Absent | Capability does not exist | Confirmed absence in documentation and product |
| Unknown | Insufficient data | Cannot verify through available sources |

**Evaluation Rules:**
- Score based on current state, not roadmap promises
- Consider depth of capability, not just presence/absence
- Weight user feedback more heavily than marketing claims
- Note any scores based on limited or outdated information

### SWOT Analysis Standards

Each SWOT item must include:
1. A specific, descriptive title (not generic labels)
2. Evidence from the competitive analysis
3. A confidence rating (High/Medium/Low)
4. An actionability assessment

**Balance requirement:** Each quadrant should contain 3-5 items. A SWOT with 8 strengths and 1 weakness indicates analytical bias, not competitive excellence.

### Positioning Map Methodology

**Dimension Selection Criteria:**
- Dimensions must be independent (not correlated)
- Dimensions must matter to buyers (validated by sales conversations or market research)
- Dimensions must differentiate (competitors spread across the space)

**Placement Rules:**
- Position based on evidence, not perception
- Cite at least two data points per placement
- Note trajectory (direction of movement) for each product
- Acknowledge uncertainty in placements

### Strategic Recommendation Framework

Recommendations follow the four-action model:

| Action | When to Use | Risk Level |
|--------|------------|------------|
| Invest | You lead and the market cares | Low — reinforces existing strength |
| Differentiate | White space exists with viable demand | Medium — requires validation |
| Match | Competitors lead and it costs you deals | Medium — resource intensive |
| Concede | Competing is uneconomic or off-strategy | Low — requires clear communication |

### Refresh Cycle

| Market Type | Recommended Refresh |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Fast-moving (AI, SaaS, consumer tech) | Quarterly |
| Moderate (B2B software, fintech) | Biannually |
| Stable (enterprise infrastructure, regulated industries) | Annually |
